# 古日本怀石前菜 | Kaiseki Appetizer (京都, ~1400 AD)

> ⏱ 准备 25分钟 + 烹饪 15分钟 | 💰 ~$8/份 | 🏷️ 古代名菜、日本、前菜、禅宗

> **📜 历史** — 怀石料理起源于14世纪日本室町时代的禅宗茶道。"怀石"一词源自僧侣将温热石头揣入怀中抵御饥饿的做法。千利休将茶道与简朴饮食结合，发展出以"一汁三菜"为核心的怀石料理体系。这道前菜（先付）是怀石宴席的开篇之作，以时令食材展现"旬"的美学。
> **📜 History** — *Kaiseki cuisine originated in 14th-century Muromachi-period Japan from Zen Buddhist tea ceremony. The word "kaiseki" derives from monks tucking warm stones into their robes to stave off hunger. Sen no Rikyu merged tea ceremony with austere dining, developing the kaiseki system centered on "one soup, three sides." This appetizer (sakizuke) opens the kaiseki feast, showcasing seasonal ingredients and the aesthetic of "shun" (peak season).*

## 食材 | Ingredients

| 食材 | Ingredient | 用量 | Amount |
|------|-----------|------|--------|
| 豆腐（绢豆腐） | Silken tofu | 1块（约300克） | 1 block (~10.5 oz) |
| 山药（磨泥） | Yamaimo yam (grated) | 1/4杯 | 1/4 cup |
| 毛豆（去壳） | Edamame (shelled) | 1/4杯 | 1/4 cup |
| 干香菇 | Dried shiitake mushrooms | 4朵 | 4 |
| 出汁（dashi） | Dashi stock | 1杯 | 1 cup |
| 淡口酱油 | Light soy sauce | 1汤匙 | 1 tbsp |
| 味醂 | Mirin | 1汤匙 | 1 tbsp |
| 山葵（现磨） | Fresh wasabi | 少许 | a dab |
| 柚子皮（切丝） | Yuzu zest (shredded) | 少许 | a pinch |

## 做法 | Directions

1. **准备出汁** — 干香菇用1杯温水泡发30分钟，取出切薄片，泡发水加入昆布煮制出汁（或使用即溶出汁粉）。
   *Soak dried shiitake in 1 cup warm water for 30 minutes. Slice thinly and reserve; use soaking water with kombu to make dashi (or use instant dashi powder).*

2. **煮浸汁** — 出汁中加入淡口酱油和味醂，小火煮沸后放入香菇片煮5分钟，关火冷却至室温。
   *Combine dashi, light soy sauce, and mirin. Simmer over low heat, add shiitake slices, and cook 5 minutes. Remove from heat and cool to room temperature.*

3. **组装** — 绢豆腐切成优雅的方块，置于小碗底部，铺上煮好的香菇片和毛豆，浇上浸汁。
   *Cut silken tofu into elegant cubes. Place in small serving bowls. Top with cooked shiitake and edamame, then ladle the simmered broth over.*

4. **点缀** — 顶部放一小撮磨好的山葵，以柚子皮丝装饰。
   *Place a small dab of freshly grated wasabi on top and garnish with shredded yuzu zest.*

## 历史注解 | Historical Notes

- 怀石料理遵循"五味五色五法"原则：甜酸苦辣咸、白黑红黄绿、生煮烤炸蒸。
  *Kaiseki follows the principle of "five flavors, five colors, five methods": sweet-sour-bitter-spicy-salty, white-black-red-yellow-green, raw-boil-grill-fry-steam.*

- 室町时代的怀石非常简朴，仅有一碗米饭、一碗味噌汤和一碟腌菜，今天的豪华形式是江户时代后的发展。
  *Muromachi-era kaiseki was extremely austere — just rice, miso soup, and pickles. Today's elaborate form developed after the Edo period.*

- 器皿的选择与食物同等重要，每一道菜的碗碟都经过精心挑选，体现季节感。
  *The choice of vessels is as important as the food itself; every bowl and plate is carefully selected to reflect the season.*

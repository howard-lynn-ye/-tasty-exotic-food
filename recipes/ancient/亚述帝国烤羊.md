# 亚述帝国烤羊 | Assyrian Empire Roast Lamb (亚述帝国, ~800 BC)

> ⏱ 准备 15分钟 + 腌制 2小时 + 烤制 1.5小时 | 💰 ~$12/份 | 🏷️ 古代名菜、亚述帝国、烤肉、羊肉

> **📜 历史** — 亚述帝国是美索不达米亚北部的军事强国，以残酷的征服和宏伟的宫殿著称。亚述国王亚述纳西尔帕二世在公元前879年举行了一场持续十天的宴会，招待近七万名宾客，烤羊是核心菜品。尼尼微宫殿浮雕中描绘了大规模烤肉宴会的场景，肉类搭配香草和面包供应。
> **📜 History** — *The Assyrian Empire was a northern Mesopotamian military power known for brutal conquests and grand palaces. King Ashurnasirpal II hosted a ten-day feast in 879 BC for nearly 70,000 guests, with roast lamb as the centerpiece. Palace reliefs at Nineveh depict grand feasting scenes with meat roasted alongside herbs and served with bread.*

## 食材 | Ingredients

| 食材 | Ingredient | 用量 | Amount |
|------|-----------|------|--------|
| 羊腿 | Leg of lamb | 1.5公斤 | 3.3 lbs |
| 大蒜（切片） | Garlic (sliced) | 6瓣 | 6 cloves |
| 小茴香粉 | Ground cumin | 1汤匙 | 1 tbsp |
| 芫荽籽（捣碎） | Coriander seeds (crushed) | 1汤匙 | 1 tbsp |
| 橄榄油 | Olive oil | 3汤匙 | 3 tbsp |
| 石榴汁 | Pomegranate juice | 1/2杯 | 1/2 cup |
| 盐 | Salt | 2茶匙 | 2 tsp |
| 新鲜薄荷 | Fresh mint | 一小把 | a handful |

---

## 做法 | Directions

1. **腌制** — 在羊腿上用刀尖戳洞，塞入蒜片。混合小茴香、芫荽籽、橄榄油和盐，均匀涂抹羊腿，浇上石榴汁，冷藏腌制2小时或过夜。
   *Pierce the lamb leg with a knife tip and insert garlic slices. Mix cumin, coriander seeds, olive oil, and salt; rub evenly over the lamb. Pour pomegranate juice over it, refrigerate and marinate for 2 hours or overnight.*

2. **烤制** — 烤箱预热至180°C（350°F），羊腿放入烤盘，盖铝箔纸烤1小时，去箔再烤30分钟至表面焦脆。
   *Preheat oven to 180°C (350°F). Place lamb in a roasting pan, cover with foil, and roast for 1 hour. Remove foil and roast 30 more minutes until the surface is crispy.*

3. **静置切片** — 出炉后静置15分钟，切片装盘，撒薄荷叶装饰，淋上烤盘汁液。
   *Rest for 15 minutes after removing from oven. Slice, arrange on a platter, garnish with mint leaves, and drizzle with pan juices.*

## 历史注解 | Historical Notes

- 亚述纳西尔帕二世的宴会铭文列出了一万只绵羊和一千只肥牛，是已知最大的古代宴会记录。
  *Ashurnasirpal II's feast inscription lists 10,000 sheep and 1,000 fattened cattle — the largest ancient banquet on record.*

- 亚述人将石榴视为神圣水果，宫殿装饰中频繁出现石榴图案。
  *Assyrians considered pomegranates sacred; pomegranate motifs appear frequently in palace decorations.*

# 犹太逾越节面包 | Jewish Matzo (古代以色列, ~1500 BC)

> ⏱ 准备 10分钟 + 烹饪 5分钟 | 💰 ~$1/份 | 🏷️ 古代名菜、犹太、面饼、素食

> **📜 历史** — Matzo（玛佐）是人类最古老的面包之一，其历史可追溯至约公元前1500年的出埃及记。据《圣经·出埃及记》记载，以色列人仓促逃离埃及时来不及等面团发酵，只能将无酵面团烤成扁饼。此后每年逾越节（Passover）期间，犹太人都食用无酵饼以纪念祖先的苦难和自由。
> **📜 History** — *Matzo is one of humanity's oldest breads, dating to the Exodus around 1500 BC. According to the Book of Exodus, the Israelites fled Egypt in such haste that they could not wait for their dough to rise, baking it flat and unleavened. Every year during Passover, Jews eat matzo to commemorate their ancestors' suffering and liberation.*

## 食材 | Ingredients

| 食材 | Ingredient | 用量 | Amount |
|------|-----------|------|--------|
| 中筋面粉 | All-purpose flour | 2杯 | 2 cups |
| 水 | Water | 3/4杯 | 3/4 cup |
| 盐 | Salt | 1/2茶匙 | 1/2 tsp |

---

## 做法 | Directions

1. **和面（限时18分钟）** — 将面粉和盐混合，加入水，快速揉成光滑面团。注意：从水接触面粉到进入烤箱，传统上不得超过18分钟，否则面团会开始发酵。
   *Mix flour and salt, add water, and quickly knead into a smooth dough. Note: Tradition dictates no more than 18 minutes from water touching flour to entering the oven, preventing any fermentation.*

2. **擀面** — 将面团分成8份，每份擀成极薄的圆片（约2毫米厚），用叉子在表面均匀戳洞防止鼓泡。
   *Divide dough into 8 pieces. Roll each paper-thin (about 2mm). Prick the surface evenly with a fork to prevent puffing.*

3. **烤制** — 烤箱预热至最高温度（250°C/475°F），将面片放在预热的烤盘上，烤2-4分钟至表面出现棕色斑点。
   *Preheat oven to maximum (250°C/475°F). Place dough on a preheated baking sheet and bake 2-4 minutes until brown spots appear.*

4. **冷却** — 取出放在架子上冷却，Matzo会变得酥脆。密封保存可存放数周。
   *Remove to a cooling rack. Matzo will crisp up as it cools. Store in airtight container for weeks.*

## 历史注解 | Historical Notes

- "18分钟"规则来自犹太律法（Halakha），拉比们认定面团在18分钟后开始自然发酵。
  *The "18-minute rule" comes from Jewish law (Halakha); rabbis determined that dough begins to naturally ferment after 18 minutes.*

- 逾越节期间犹太家庭必须清除家中所有发酵物（chametz），Matzo成为唯一允许的面食。
  *During Passover, Jewish families must remove all leavened products (chametz) from home; matzo becomes the only permitted bread.*

- 考古学家在死海古卷遗址附近发现了约2000年前的碳化面饼残片，形态与现代Matzo几乎一致。
  *Archaeologists found carbonized flatbread fragments near the Dead Sea Scrolls site, nearly identical in form to modern matzo, dating back about 2,000 years.*

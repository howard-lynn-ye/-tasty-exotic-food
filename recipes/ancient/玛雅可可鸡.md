# 玛雅可可鸡 | Mayan Chocolate Chicken (玛雅文明, ~600 AD)

> ⏱ 准备 20分钟 + 烹饪 45分钟 | 💰 ~$8/份 | 🏷️ 古代名菜、玛雅、鸡肉、可可

> **📜 历史** — 玛雅人将可可视为"众神之食"，可可豆不仅是饮料原料，还被用作货币。在玛雅古典期（约250-900 AD），贵族和祭司将可可与辣椒、玉米混合制作神圣饮品。考古证据表明玛雅人也将可可用于烹饪肉类——这是墨西哥"摩尔酱"（Mole）的远古原型。蒂卡尔和帕伦克的宫殿遗址中出土了带有可可残留的烹饪陶器，证明了可可在玛雅宫廷料理中的重要地位。
> **📜 History** — *The Maya revered cacao as the "food of the gods"; cacao beans served as both beverage ingredient and currency. During the Classic Maya period (c. 250-900 AD), nobles and priests mixed cacao with chili and corn for sacred drinks. Archaeological evidence shows Maya also used cacao in cooking meat — an ancient prototype of Mexican Mole sauce. Palace sites at Tikal and Palenque have yielded cooking vessels with cacao residue, proving cacao's importance in Maya court cuisine.*

## 食材 | Ingredients

| 食材 | Ingredient | 用量 | Amount |
|------|-----------|------|--------|
| 鸡腿（带骨） | Chicken legs (bone-in) | 4只 | 4 pieces |
| 无糖可可粉 | Unsweetened cocoa powder | 2汤匙 | 2 tbsp |
| 干辣椒（去籽） | Dried chili (deseeded) | 2个 | 2 |
| 番茄（切碎） | Tomato (chopped) | 2个 | 2 |

| 植物油 | Vegetable oil | 2汤匙 | 2 tbsp |
| 盐 | Salt | 适量 | to taste |

## 做法 | Directions

1. **煎鸡** — 鸡腿撒盐，锅中加油大火煎至两面金黄（约每面3分钟），取出备用。
   *Salt the chicken legs. Heat oil in a pan and sear over high heat until golden on both sides (about 3 minutes each side). Remove and set aside.*

2. **制酱** — 同锅中火炒洋葱、大蒜和干辣椒至软，加番茄炒至出汁。加入可可粉、肉桂、小茴香和蜂蜜搅拌均匀，倒入鸡高汤煮沸。
   *In the same pan over medium heat, sauté onion, garlic, and dried chili until soft. Add tomato and cook until juicy. Stir in cocoa powder, cinnamon, cumin, and honey. Pour in chicken broth and bring to a boil.*

3. **炖煮** — 鸡腿放回酱汁中，转小火加盖炖煮35-40分钟至鸡肉熟透入味。
   *Return chicken to the sauce, reduce heat, cover, and simmer 35-40 minutes until chicken is cooked through and flavors meld.*

4. **装盘** — 鸡腿盛入盘中，浇上浓稠的可可酱汁，可撒少许可可粉和芝麻装饰。
   *Plate the chicken legs, spoon the rich cocoa sauce over them, and garnish with a dusting of cocoa powder and sesame seeds.*

## 历史注解 | Historical Notes

- 玛雅象形文字中"ka-ka-w"就是可可的读音，这个词最终演变为英语的"cacao"和"cocoa"。
  *The Maya glyph "ka-ka-w" is the pronunciation for cacao — the word that eventually became English "cacao" and "cocoa."*

- 玛雅人相信可可树生长在冥界与人间的交界处，可可饮品是沟通神灵的媒介。
  *The Maya believed cacao trees grew at the boundary between the underworld and the human world; cacao beverages were a medium for communicating with the gods.*

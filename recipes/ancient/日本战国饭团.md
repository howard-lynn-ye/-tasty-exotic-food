## 历史背景 Historical Background

饭团的历史可上溯至弥生时代，但在战国时代成为武士的重要战场口粮。足轻（步兵）和武士在行军中携带用竹皮包裹的盐握饭团，既便携又耐存。战国饭团不用海苔（海苔包饭团是江户时代以后的事），以梅干或味噌为芯，盐渍外层防腐。

Onigiri dates back to the Yayoi period, but became crucial military field rations during the Sengoku era. Foot soldiers (ashigaru) and samurai carried salt-pressed rice balls wrapped in bamboo sheaths — portable and durable. Sengoku onigiri did not use nori (seaweed wrapping came after the Edo period). They were filled with umeboshi or miso, with a salted exterior for preservation.

## 步骤 Steps

### 第一步 Step 1 — 煮饭 Cook rice
米洗净浸泡30分钟，按正常水量煮熟。煮好后趁热用饭勺翻松，不要压碎米粒。稍晾至手可触碰的温度。
Wash rice and soak 30 minutes. Cook with standard water ratio. Once done, fluff with a rice paddle while hot — do not crush grains. Cool slightly until handleable.

### 第二步 Step 2 — 握制 Form onigiri
手沾盐水（防粘且调味），取约一碗量的热饭在掌心。中间放入一颗梅干或一小团味噌，双手呈三角形用力握紧定型，反复按压3-4次使饭团紧实。
Wet hands with salted water (prevents sticking and seasons). Take about one bowl of hot rice in your palm. Press an umeboshi or a dab of miso into the center. Cup hands into a triangle and press firmly to shape, compacting 3-4 times until solid.

### 第三步 Step 3 — 包裹 Wrap
做好的饭团用竹叶或保鲜膜紧紧包裹。战国武士会在出征前一天做好，室温下可保存一天。现代建议当天食用。
Wrap finished onigiri tightly in bamboo leaves or plastic wrap. Sengoku warriors made these the day before marching — they kept at room temperature for a day. Modern recommendation: eat same day.

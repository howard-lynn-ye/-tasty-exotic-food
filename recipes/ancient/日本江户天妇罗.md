# 日本江户天妇罗 | Edo Period Tempura (江户时代, ~1700 AD)

> ⏱ 准备 20分钟 + 烹饪 15分钟 | 💰 ~$8/份 | 🏷️ 古代名菜、江户时代、油炸、海鲜

> **📜 历史** — 天妇罗的起源与16世纪到达日本的葡萄牙传教士有关。"Tempura"一词可能源自葡萄牙语"tempero"（调味）或拉丁语"tempora"（四季斋期）。到江户时代，天妇罗已完全日本化，成为江户（东京）街头最受欢迎的小吃。路边摊贩将新鲜虾和蔬菜裹上薄面糊油炸，蘸天汁（萝卜泥+酱油高汤）食用。德川家康据传极爱天妇罗，甚至有传说他因食用过量天妇罗而导致身体不适。
> **📜 History** — *Tempura's origins trace to Portuguese missionaries who arrived in Japan in the 16th century. "Tempura" likely derives from Portuguese "tempero" (seasoning) or Latin "tempora" (Ember Days fasting periods). By the Edo period, tempura had been fully Japanized, becoming the most popular street food in Edo (Tokyo). Street vendors fried fresh shrimp and vegetables in thin batter, served with tentsuyu dipping sauce. Tokugawa Ieyasu was reportedly so fond of tempura that legend claims he fell ill from overindulging.*

## 做法 | Directions

1. **备天汁** — 将高汤、酱油和味醂混合煮沸后冷却。食用时加入萝卜泥搅匀。
   *Combine dashi, soy sauce, and mirin; bring to a boil then cool. Add grated daikon when serving.*

2. **制面糊** — 鸡蛋打散加入冰水搅匀，筛入面粉，用筷子轻轻搅拌几下即可（面糊应有颗粒感，切勿过度搅拌，否则面筋过多影响酥脆度）。
   *Beat the egg into ice water. Sift in flour and stir lightly with chopsticks just a few times (batter should be lumpy — do NOT overmix or gluten will develop and reduce crispness).*

3. **炸制** — 油加热至340-360°F（170-180°C）。食材拍少量干面粉，裹上面糊，逐一放入油中炸。虾炸2-3分钟，蔬菜炸1-2分钟，至面糊金黄酥脆。沥油放在架子上。
   *Heat oil to 340-360°F (170-180°C). Dust ingredients lightly with flour, dip in batter, and fry one by one. Shrimp: 2-3 minutes; vegetables: 1-2 minutes, until batter is golden and crisp. Drain on a wire rack.*

4. **摆盘上桌** — 天妇罗摆在铺有吸油纸的盘中，配天汁蘸碟趁热食用。酥脆感稍纵即逝，必须现炸现吃。
   *Arrange tempura on a plate lined with absorbent paper. Serve with tentsuyu dipping sauce. Eat immediately — crispness is fleeting.*

## 历史注解 | Historical Notes

- 江户时代天妇罗是庶民美食，路边摊售价极低，与寿司、荞麦面并称"江户三味"。
  *Edo-period tempura was common street food sold cheaply from stalls, alongside sushi and soba as the "three flavors of Edo."*

- 天妇罗面糊的关键是冰水和不过度搅拌——这两个技巧保证了日式天妇罗独特的轻薄酥脆。
  *The keys to tempura batter are ice-cold water and minimal mixing — these two techniques ensure the signature light, shatteringly crisp coating.*

- 江户时代炸油主要使用芝麻油，赋予天妇罗独特的坚果香气。现代多用植物油。
  *Edo-period frying oil was primarily sesame oil, giving tempura a distinctive nutty aroma. Modern versions mostly use vegetable oil.*

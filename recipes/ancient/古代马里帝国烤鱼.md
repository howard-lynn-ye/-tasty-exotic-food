# 古代马里帝国烤鱼 | Mali Empire Grilled Fish

> **朝代/时期 Dynasty/Era:** 马里帝国 Mali Empire (~1300AD)
> **发源地 Origin:** 尼日尔河流域 Niger River Valley
> **类型 Type:** 烤鱼 Grilled Fish

---

## 典故 Historical Background

马里帝国在曼萨·穆萨统治时期达到鼎盛，是当时世界上最富有的帝国之一。尼日尔河是帝国的生命线，河中丰富的鱼类资源养育了沿岸居民。渔民以传统方式在河岸边烤鱼，以乳木果油和当地香料调味，形成了独特的萨赫勒风味。烤鱼在廷巴克图的集市上是最受欢迎的街头食物，学者与商人在此边吃边交流。

The Mali Empire reached its zenith under Mansa Musa, becoming one of the world's wealthiest empires. The Niger River was the empire's lifeline, its abundant fish sustaining riverside communities. Fishermen grilled fish on riverbanks in traditional fashion, seasoned with shea butter and local spices, creating a distinctive Sahelian flavor. Grilled fish was the most popular street food in Timbuktu's markets, where scholars and merchants ate while exchanging ideas.

## 做法 Preparation

1. **处理鱼 Prepare fish:** 整鱼去鳞去内脏洗净，两侧各划三刀以便入味。Scale and gut the fish, wash clean, score three cuts on each side for seasoning penetration.
2. **制腌料 Make marinade:** 大蒜与生姜捣成泥，与柠檬汁、盐和辣椒粉混合成腌酱。Pound garlic and ginger into paste, mix with lemon juice, salt, and chili powder into a marinade.
3. **腌鱼 Marinate:** 将腌酱涂抹鱼身内外及刀口处，静置一个时辰。Rub marinade all over the fish, inside and into the cuts, rest for 1 hour.
4. **备火 Prepare fire:** 以硬木生炭火，待炭面覆白灰无明火时即可烤制。Build a hardwood charcoal fire, wait until coals are covered with white ash and free of open flame.
5. **烤鱼 Grill:** 鱼表面刷乳木果油防粘，置于铁丝网架上以炭火慢烤，每面约一刻钟，中途再刷油保持湿润。Brush fish with shea butter to prevent sticking, place on a wire rack over coals, grill about 15 minutes per side, basting with more butter midway.
6. **制酱上桌 Make sauce and serve:** 洋葱与番茄切碎，以少许乳木果油炒制成辣番茄酱。烤鱼盛于芭蕉叶上，淋番茄酱，配木薯或小米食用。Chop onion and tomatoes, fry in shea butter into a spicy tomato sauce. Serve grilled fish on banana leaves, top with tomato sauce, eat with cassava or millet.

## 备注 Notes

- 乳木果油是西非的传统烹饪油脂，赋予食物独特的坚果香气。Shea butter is West Africa's traditional cooking fat, imparting a distinctive nutty aroma to food.
- 廷巴克图的鱼市至今仍沿用中世纪的烤鱼传统，是联合国非物质文化遗产的一部分。Timbuktu's fish markets still follow medieval grilling traditions, part of UNESCO intangible cultural heritage.

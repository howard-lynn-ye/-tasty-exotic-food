# 明代狮子头 Ming Dynasty Lion's Head Meatball

> **时代 Era:** 明朝 Ming Dynasty · 约公元1400年 (~1400 AD)
> **地区 Region:** 金陵 Jinling (modern Nanjing)
> **类型 Type:** 炖菜 Braised Dish
> **难度 Difficulty:** ★★★☆☆

---

## 历史背景 Historical Background

狮子头的前身可追溯至隋唐时期的"葵花斩肉"，至明代已成为淮扬菜系的经典名菜。其名源于肉丸表面凹凸形似狮子头部。明代文人宴席上，狮子头常以砂锅清炖的方式呈上，配以白菜或豆腐，追求"大味至淡"的境界。

The predecessor of Lion's Head dates to the Sui-Tang "Sunflower Chopped Meat." By the Ming Dynasty it was a classic Huaiyang dish. The name comes from the meatball's textured surface resembling a lion's mane. At Ming literati banquets, it was often clear-braised in a clay pot with cabbage or tofu — pursuing the philosophy of "supreme flavor in simplicity."

## 食材 Ingredients

| 食材 Ingredient | 用量 Amount |
|---|---|
| 五花肉 Pork belly (手切粗粒 hand-chopped) | 500g |
| 娃娃菜 Baby napa cabbage | 300g |
| 生姜 Ginger (切片 sliced) | 3 片 slices |
| 黄酒 Shaoxing wine | 2 汤匙 tbsp |
| 盐 Salt | 1 茶匙 tsp |
| 淀粉 Starch | 1 汤匙 tbsp |

---

## 步骤 Steps

### 第一步 Step 1 — 制丸 Form meatballs
五花肉手工切成黄豆大小的粗粒（切勿用绞肉机），加入盐、黄酒、淀粉，顺一个方向搅拌上劲。分成4个大丸子，双手交替摔打定型。
Hand-chop the pork belly into soybean-sized pieces (do not use a grinder). Add salt, wine, and starch. Stir vigorously in one direction until sticky. Divide into 4 large balls, tossing between hands to compact.

### 第二步 Step 2 — 煎炖 Sear and braise
平底锅中火加少许油，将丸子表面煎至微黄定型。砂锅底部铺上白菜和姜片，放入丸子，加清水至没过丸子三分之二。
Sear meatballs in a lightly oiled pan over medium heat until lightly golden. Line a clay pot with cabbage and ginger slices. Place meatballs on top and add water to cover two-thirds.

### 第三步 Step 3 — 慢炖 Slow cook
大火煮沸后转最小火，加盖炖1.5-2小时，中途不要翻动。炖至丸子酥软、汤汁浓白即可。
Bring to a boil, then reduce to the lowest heat. Cover and braise 1.5-2 hours without disturbing. Done when meatballs are meltingly tender and broth is milky white.

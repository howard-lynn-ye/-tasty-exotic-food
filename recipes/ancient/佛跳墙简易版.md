> **📜 历史** — 佛跳墙是中国菜中的"王者"，创于清光绪年间福州官府菜。传说此菜香气浓郁到"佛闻弃禅跳墙来"——连修行的和尚闻到都要翻墙来吃。传统版用鲍鱼、海参、鱼翅等名贵食材，极其昂贵。这个简易版用美国超市能买到的食材还原80%的风味，不用花$200。
>
> **📜 History** — *Buddha Jumps Over the Wall is the "king" of Chinese cuisine, created in 1876 at a Fuzhou official's banquet. Legend says its aroma was so intoxicating that "even Buddha would abandon his meditation and leap over the wall to taste it." The traditional version uses abalone, sea cucumber, and shark fin — extremely expensive. This simplified version recreates 80% of the flavor with American supermarket ingredients, no $200 required.*

| 食材 | Ingredient | 用量 / Amount |
|------|-----------|---------------|

## 做法 | Directions

### 1. 预处理 | Prep All
香菇泡发。鸡腿和排骨分别焯水5分钟去血沫。虾洗净。

Soak shiitake mushrooms. Blanch chicken and ribs separately for 5 minutes. Clean shrimp.

### 2. 分层摆放 | Layer
在深砂锅或 Dutch oven 中，底部放猪蹄/猪皮（提供胶质），上面依次放排骨、鸡腿、香菇、干贝、虾，撒上枸杞和红枣。

In a deep pot or Dutch oven, place pork trotter at bottom (for gelatin). Layer ribs, chicken, shiitake, scallops, shrimp. Scatter goji berries and red dates.

### 3. 炖煮 | Braise
加入黄酒、蚝油、姜葱，加水没过食材。大火烧开后转最小火，加盖炖2小时。（Instant Pot: 高压45分钟）

Add wine, oyster sauce, ginger, scallion. Add water to cover. Boil, then lowest heat, covered, 2 hours. (Instant Pot: High pressure 45 min.)

### 4. 调味上桌 | Season & Serve
加盐和白胡椒粉。汤头应该已经浓郁鲜美得不可思议。直接连锅端上桌。

Season with salt and white pepper. The broth should be unbelievably rich. Serve the pot at the table.

## 历史注解 | Historical Notes

> 佛跳墙原名"福寿全"，由福州厨师郑春发在同治年间创制。据《闽都别记》记载，一位文人品尝后赋诗："坛启荤香飘四邻，佛闻弃禅跳墙来。" 从此改名"佛跳墙"。正宗版本需要鲍鱼、海参、鱼翅、花胶等十几种名贵食材，炖煮3天3夜。我们这个简易版保留了多种食材分层长时间炖煮的核心理念，用普通食材还原了那种"所有鲜味汇聚在一锅"的震撼。
>
> *Originally named "Fù Shòu Quán" (Complete Fortune & Longevity), created by Fuzhou chef Zheng Chunfa. A poet tasted it and wrote: "When the jar opens, its aroma floats to all neighbors / Even Buddha forsakes meditation to leap over the wall." The authentic version requires 3 days of simmering with abalone, sea cucumber, and shark fin. Our simplified version preserves the core concept — multiple ingredients layered and slow-braised until all their umami converges in one extraordinary broth.*

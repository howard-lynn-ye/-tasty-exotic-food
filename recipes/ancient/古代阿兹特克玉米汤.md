# 古代阿兹特克玉米汤 | Aztec Corn Soup

> **朝代/时期 Dynasty/Era:** 阿兹特克帝国 Aztec Empire (~1300AD)
> **发源地 Origin:** 特诺奇提特兰（今墨西哥城） Tenochtitlan (modern Mexico City)
> **类型 Type:** 汤/主食 Soup / Staple

---

## 典故 Historical Background

玉米是阿兹特克文明的神圣作物，被称为"众神的恩赐"。阿兹特克人发明了以石灰水浸泡玉米的"尼斯塔马利化"工艺，极大提升了玉米的营养价值。波索莱汤（Pozole）是阿兹特克帝国最重要的仪式性食物，在祭祀太阳神的重大节日中，以巨大的石锅烹煮，全城共享。此汤以膨胀的玉米粒为主体，汤底浓郁鲜美。

Corn was the sacred crop of Aztec civilization, called "the gift of the gods." The Aztecs invented nixtamalization — soaking corn in lime water — dramatically increasing its nutritional value. Pozole was the empire's most important ritual food, cooked in massive stone pots during festivals honoring the sun god and shared city-wide. The soup features bloomed hominy corn kernels in a rich, savory broth.

| 青柠 Lime | 2个 2 whole |

## 做法 Preparation

1. **尼斯塔马利化 Nixtamalize:** 干玉米粒与石灰加水煮沸，离火浸泡一夜，次日搓洗去皮得膨发的玉米仁。Boil dried corn with cal and water, remove from heat and soak overnight, next day rub off skins to obtain bloomed hominy.
2. **煮火鸡 Cook turkey:** 火鸡肉放入大陶锅加水煮至熟烂，取出撕成粗丝，汤汁留用。Simmer turkey in a large clay pot until tender, shred into strips, reserve the broth.
3. **制辣椒酱 Make chili sauce:** 干辣椒去籽，在石板上轻烤至出香，与南瓜籽、大蒜一同在石磨（molcajete）中研成糊状。Deseed dried chilies, lightly toast on a stone griddle until fragrant, grind with pumpkin seeds and garlic in a stone mortar (molcajete) into a paste.
4. **炒酱 Cook sauce:** 辣椒糊放入锅中炒至颜色变深出香，倒入火鸡汤搅匀。Fry the chili paste until darkened and fragrant, pour in turkey broth and stir.
5. **合炖 Combine and stew:** 加入膨发玉米仁与牛至，小火炖煮至玉米粒完全绽开如花朵状，汤汁浓稠。Add hominy and oregano, simmer on low until corn kernels fully bloom like flowers and broth thickens.
6. **上桌 Serve:** 盛入陶碗，铺上火鸡丝，配萝卜丝、青柠角，根据喜好加辣。Ladle into clay bowls, top with shredded turkey, garnish with radish and lime wedges, add chili to taste.

## 备注 Notes

- 尼斯塔马利化是美洲对世界食品科学的伟大贡献，释放了玉米中被锁住的烟酸（维生素B3）。Nixtamalization is the Americas' great contribution to food science, releasing locked niacin (vitamin B3) from corn.
- 现代墨西哥的波索莱汤通常使用猪肉，但阿兹特克原版使用火鸡。Modern Mexican pozole typically uses pork, but the Aztec original used turkey.

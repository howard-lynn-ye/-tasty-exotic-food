# 泰国素可泰青咖喱 | Sukhothai Green Curry (~1300AD)

> ⏱ 准备20分+烹饪25分 | 💰~$10/份 | 🏷️ 古代名菜、素可泰王朝

> **📜 历史** — 素可泰王朝是泰国烹饪的黄金时期，青咖喱以新鲜青辣椒和椰奶为基底，展现了东南亚香料的精髓。
> **📜 History** — *The Sukhothai era was Thailand's culinary golden age; green curry with fresh green chilies and coconut milk showcases the essence of Southeast Asian spices.*

---

## 食材 | Ingredients

| 食材 | Ingredient | 用量 / Amount |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| 鸡胸肉 | Chicken breast | 300g / 10oz |
| 青咖喱酱 | Green curry paste | 45g / 3 tbsp |
| 椰奶 | Coconut milk | 400ml / 1 can |
| 茄子 | Eggplant | 1个 / 1 small |
| 罗勒 | Thai basil | 一把 / 1 handful |
| 鱼露 | Fish sauce | 15ml / 1 tbsp |

---

## 做法 | Directions

### 1. 炒酱 | Fry Paste

锅中加少许椰奶炒香青咖喱酱至出油。
Fry green curry paste in a splash of coconut milk until fragrant and oil separates.

### 2. 炖煮 | Simmer

加鸡肉块和茄子，倒入剩余椰奶，中火煮15分钟。
Add chicken pieces and eggplant, pour in remaining coconut milk, simmer 15 minutes.

### 3. 调味 | Season

加鱼露调味，撒上罗勒叶，配米饭食用。
Season with fish sauce, top with Thai basil, serve over steamed rice.

## 要点 | Tips

| # | 要点 | Tip |
|---|------|-----|
| 1 | 先用椰奶上层浓厚部分炒酱，更易逼出咖喱油香 | Use the thick cream from the top of the coconut can to fry the paste for better aroma |
| 2 | 鸡肉切薄片而非大块，15分钟内即可完全熟透 | Slice chicken thin rather than chunky so it cooks through within 15 minutes |
| 3 | 罗勒叶关火后再放入，余温刚好激发香气而不变黑 | Add basil leaves after turning off heat; residual warmth releases fragrance without blackening |

## 历史注解 | Historical Notes

素可泰碑文（1292年兰甘亨大帝石碑）描绘了"水中有鱼、田中有稻"的富庶景象，但青咖喱的具体起源难以精确考证。辣椒实际上在16世纪才由葡萄牙人带入东南亚，因此素可泰时期的"咖喱"更可能以胡椒和本地香料为基底。现代青咖喱酱的配方——青辣椒、香茅、南姜、柠檬叶——是数百年文化交流的结晶。

The Sukhothai inscription (King Ramkhamhaeng's 1292 stele) depicts an abundant land of "fish in the water, rice in the fields," but the precise origin of green curry is hard to pin down. Chilies were not introduced to Southeast Asia until the 16th century by the Portuguese, so Sukhothai-era "curry" likely used pepper and local aromatics. The modern green curry paste formula -- green chilies, lemongrass, galangal, kaffir lime leaves -- is the product of centuries of cultural exchange.

# 古罗马士兵粥 | Roman Legionary Porridge (罗马帝国, ~100 AD)
<p align="center">  <img src="../../images/ancient/古罗马士兵粥.svg" width="500" alt="古罗马士兵粥"></p>

> ⏱ 准备 5分钟 + 烹饪 25分钟 | 💰 ~$2/份 | 🏷️ 古代名菜、古罗马、粥品、军粮

> **📜 历史** — Puls（普尔斯）是古罗马军团士兵的标准口粮，比面包更古老，是罗马文明最基础的食物。罗马人甚至因此被称为"吃粥的人"（pultiphagonides）。这种以斯佩尔特小麦为基础的浓粥维持了从不列颠到叙利亚的数十万军团士兵的战斗力，是罗马帝国扩张的幕后功臣。
> **📜 History** — *Puls was the standard ration of Roman legionary soldiers, older than bread and the most fundamental food of Roman civilization. Romans were even nicknamed "porridge eaters" (pultiphagonides). This thick porridge, based on spelt wheat, sustained hundreds of thousands of soldiers from Britain to Syria — the unsung hero behind Rome's imperial expansion.*

## 食材 | Ingredients

| 食材 | Ingredient | 用量 | Amount |
|------|-----------|------|--------|
| 斯佩尔特小麦（碾碎） | Cracked spelt wheat | 1杯 | 1 cup |
| 水 | Water | 3杯 | 3 cups |
| 橄榄油 | Olive oil | 2汤匙 | 2 tbsp |
| 盐 | Salt | 1茶匙 | 1 tsp |
| 蜂蜜（可选） | Honey (optional) | 1汤匙 | 1 tbsp |
| 干酪（磨碎） | Hard cheese (grated) | 2汤匙 | 2 tbsp |
| 新鲜香草（如牛至、百里香） | Fresh herbs (oregano, thyme) | 少许 | a handful |

---

## 做法 | Directions

1. **煮粥** — 水加盐煮沸，缓缓加入碾碎的斯佩尔特小麦，不断搅拌防止结块。
   *Bring salted water to a boil. Slowly pour in cracked spelt while stirring constantly to prevent lumps.*

2. **慢熬** — 转小火，持续搅拌熬煮20-25分钟至粥变得浓稠如热麦片。如过于浓稠可加少量热水调整。
   *Reduce heat to low. Stir frequently and cook 20-25 minutes until thick like hot oatmeal. Add a splash of hot water if too thick.*

3. **调味** — 关火后淋入橄榄油，拌入切碎的新鲜香草，撒上磨碎的干酪。
   *Remove from heat, drizzle with olive oil, stir in chopped fresh herbs, and sprinkle with grated cheese.*

4. **装盘** — 盛入碗中，可额外淋蜂蜜做甜味版本，或保持咸味版搭配橄榄和面包。
   *Serve in bowls. Optionally drizzle honey for a sweet version, or keep savory and serve with olives and bread on the side.*

## 历史注解 | Historical Notes

- 罗马军团士兵每天的口粮配给约800克小麦，他们用随身携带的石磨在营地中碾磨谷物。
  *Each Roman legionary received about 800g of wheat daily; they ground grain at camp using portable stone mills (querns).*

- 加图（Cato the Elder）在《农业志》中记录了Puls的食谱，包括加入奶酪、蛋和蜂蜜的"高级版"。
  *Cato the Elder recorded puls recipes in "De Agri Cultura," including an "upscale" version with cheese, eggs, and honey.*

- 随着面包烘焙技术的普及，Puls逐渐从主食降为穷人食物，但它始终是罗马身份认同的象征。
  *As bread-baking technology spread, puls gradually declined from staple to poor man's food, yet it remained a symbol of Roman identity.*

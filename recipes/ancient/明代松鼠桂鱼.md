# 明代松鼠桂鱼 | Ming Dynasty Squirrel Fish (明代, ~1500 AD)
<p align="center">  <img src="../../images/ancient/明代松鼠桂鱼.svg" width="500" alt="明代松鼠桂鱼"></p>

> ⏱ 准备 25分钟 + 烹饪 15分钟 | 💰 ~$10/份 | 🏷️ 古代名菜、明代、苏帮菜、油炸

> **📜 历史** — 松鼠桂鱼是苏州名菜，相传始于明代，因鱼炸后形似松鼠翘尾而得名。乾隆皇帝南巡苏州时对此菜赞不绝口的故事虽属传说，但此菜在明清时期确已是江南名厨的看家本领。刀工是关键：整鱼剞花刀后炸制，鱼肉绽开如松果，浇上酸甜卤汁发出"吱吱"声响，色香味俱全。
> **📜 History** — *Squirrel Fish is a famous Suzhou dish, believed to originate in the Ming Dynasty, named because the deep-fried fish curls up to resemble a squirrel's raised tail. While the story of Emperor Qianlong praising it during his Jiangnan tour is likely legend, this dish was certainly a signature skill of Jiangnan master chefs during the Ming-Qing era. Knife work is crucial: the whole fish is scored in a crosshatch pattern before frying, causing the flesh to bloom like a pinecone, then doused with sweet-and-sour sauce that sizzles dramatically.*

## 做法 | Directions

1. **处理鱼** — 鱼去鳞去内脏，从背部片开鱼肉（保持尾部相连），去除主骨。在鱼肉上切交叉花刀（斜刀切到鱼皮但不切断），撒盐，均匀拍上玉米淀粉。
   *Scale and gut the fish. Fillet from the back, keeping the tail connected, and remove the spine. Score the flesh in a crosshatch pattern (cut diagonally down to the skin without cutting through). Season with salt and coat evenly with cornstarch.*

2. **炸鱼** — 油加热至350°F（175°C），提着鱼尾将鱼放入油锅，用勺将热油反复浇淋鱼身，炸4-5分钟至金黄酥脆、鱼肉绽开如松果。取出沥油，摆盘。
   *Heat oil to 350°F (175°C). Holding the tail, lower the fish into the oil. Ladle hot oil over the fish repeatedly. Fry 4-5 minutes until golden and crispy with flesh blooming open. Drain and plate.*

3. **制酸甜汁** — 另起锅，将番茄酱、醋、糖和水煮沸，加入水淀粉勾芡至浓稠，放入青豆和松子（如用）。
   *In a separate pan, bring ketchup, vinegar, sugar, and water to a boil. Add cornstarch slurry to thicken. Stir in peas and pine nuts (if using).*

4. **浇汁上桌** — 将滚烫的酸甜汁浇在炸好的鱼上（会发出吱吱声），立即上桌。
   *Pour the piping hot sauce over the fried fish (it will sizzle dramatically). Serve immediately.*

## 历史注解 | Historical Notes

- 明代苏州是全国最富庶的城市之一，"苏帮菜"以精致刀工和甜口著称。
  *Ming Dynasty Suzhou was one of China's wealthiest cities; "Su-style cuisine" is renowned for exquisite knife work and slightly sweet flavors.*

- 传统用鳜鱼（mandarin fish），因其肉质紧实、少刺，最适合此种刀工和炸制。
  *The traditional choice is mandarin fish (guiyu) for its firm, low-bone flesh — ideal for this scoring and frying technique.*

- 番茄酱是近代替代品，明代原版酸甜汁可能使用梅子醋和麦芽糖。
  *Ketchup is a modern substitute; the Ming-era sauce likely used plum vinegar and maltose.*

# 北宋汴京灌汤包 | Northern Song Soup Dumplings (北宋, ~1050 AD)

> ⏱ 准备 60分钟 + 烹饪 15分钟 | 💰 ~$5/份 | 🏷️ 古代名菜、宋朝、面食、肉类

> **📜 历史** — 灌汤包的前身可追溯至北宋都城汴京（开封）的"山洞梅花包子"。《东京梦华录》记载汴京酒楼中售卖"灌浆馒头"，即皮薄汁多的肉包，食客需"先开窗、后喝汤、再吃肉"。北宋时期面食技艺达到巅峰，汴京七十二家正店中的包子铺竞相比拼皮薄馅多。灌汤包的秘密在于肉馅中加入冷却凝固的肉皮冻，蒸制时肉冻融化成汤汁，这一技法千年未变。
> **📜 History** — *The predecessor of soup dumplings can be traced to "Mountain Cave Plum Blossom Baozi" in the Northern Song capital Bianjing (Kaifeng). "Dreams of Splendor of the Eastern Capital" records "jelly-filled steamed buns" sold in Bianjing restaurants — thin-skinned, juice-filled meat buns eaten by "first opening a window, then sipping the soup, then eating the meat." The secret lies in adding chilled meat-skin jelly (aspic) to the filling, which melts into soup when steamed — a technique unchanged for a millennium.*

## 食材 | Ingredients

| 食材 | Ingredient | 用量 | Amount |
|------|-----------|------|--------|
| 中筋面粉 | All-purpose flour | 2杯 | 2 cups |
| 温水 | Warm water | 3/4杯 | 3/4 cup |
| 猪肉末 | Ground pork | 250g | 8 oz |
| 猪皮冻（切碎） | Pork aspic (chopped) | 100g | 3.5 oz |
| 姜末 | Minced ginger | 1茶匙 | 1 tsp |

| 香油 | Sesame oil | 1茶匙 | 1 tsp |
| 黄酒 | Shaoxing wine | 1汤匙 | 1 tbsp |

| 镇江香醋（蘸料） | Chinkiang vinegar (dipping) | 适量 | to taste |
| 姜丝（蘸料） | Ginger julienne (dipping) | 适量 | to taste |

## 做法 | Directions

1. **和面与拌馅** — 面粉加温水揉成光滑面团，盖湿布醒30分钟。猪肉末加酱油、黄酒、姜末、葱花、香油、盐和白胡椒拌匀，再拌入切碎的猪皮冻。
   *Knead flour with warm water into a smooth dough, cover with damp cloth and rest 30 minutes. Mix ground pork with soy sauce, wine, ginger, scallion, sesame oil, salt, and white pepper. Fold in chopped pork aspic.*

2. **包制** — 面团分成20个小剂子，擀成中间厚边缘薄的圆皮，每个包入一勺馅料，捏出18-20个褶子收口。
   *Divide dough into 20 pieces, roll each into a round wrapper (thicker center, thinner edges). Place a spoonful of filling in each, pleat 18-20 folds to seal.*

3. **蒸制** — 蒸笼垫上油纸或菜叶，将灌汤包间隔摆放，大火蒸8-10分钟至皮透明、汁水鼓起。配姜丝香醋蘸食。
   *Line steamer with parchment or cabbage leaves. Place dumplings with space between. Steam on high 8-10 minutes until wrappers turn translucent and plump with juice. Serve with ginger-vinegar dipping sauce.*

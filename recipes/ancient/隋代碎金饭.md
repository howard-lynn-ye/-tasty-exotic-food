# 隋代碎金饭 | Sui Golden Fried Rice (~600AD)
<p align="center">  <img src="../../images/ancient/隋代碎金饭.svg" width="500" alt="隋代碎金饭"></p>

> ⏱ 准备15分+烹饪10分 | 💰~$6/份 | 🏷️ 古代名菜、隋朝

> **📜 历史** — 隋炀帝巡幸扬州时，御厨以蛋炒饭色如碎金而名之，是扬州炒饭的前身。
> **📜 History** — *When Emperor Yang of Sui visited Yangzhou, his chef named this egg fried rice "broken gold" for its golden color — the ancestor of Yangzhou fried rice.*

---

## 食材 | Ingredients
| 食材 | Ingredient | 用量 / Amount |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| 隔夜米饭 | Day-old rice | 400g / 2 cups |
| 鸡蛋 | Eggs | 3个 / 3 |
| 葱 | Scallion | 2根 / 2 stalks |
| 盐 | Salt | 适量 / To taste |
| 猪油 | Lard | 30ml / 2 tbsp |

---

## 做法 | Directions

### 1. 炒蛋 | Scramble Eggs
大火热猪油，打入鸡蛋快速翻炒至半凝固。

Heat lard on high, crack in eggs and scramble until half-set.

### 2. 炒饭 | Fry Rice
倒入隔夜饭大火翻炒，让每粒米裹上蛋液，呈金黄色。

Add day-old rice, stir-fry vigorously so each grain is coated in egg and turns golden.

### 3. 调味 | Season
加盐调味，撒葱花翻匀出锅。

Season with salt, toss in scallions, stir evenly and serve.

---

## 要点 | Tips
| 要点 | Tip |
|------|-----|
| 隔夜饭必须完全冷透，米粒干爽才能炒出粒粒分明的效果 | Day-old rice must be fully chilled and dry for separate, distinct grains |
| 蛋液半凝固时就下饭，让蛋裹住每粒米是"碎金"的关键 | Add rice when eggs are half-set; coating each grain is the key to the "golden" effect |
| 全程大火快炒，锅气足才香，切忌中途调小火 | Keep high heat throughout for wok hei; never reduce the flame mid-cook |

## 历史注解 | Historical Notes
碎金饭之名来自其金黄剔透的外观，隋炀帝三下扬州，奢华饮食闻名于世，碎金饭便是皇家御膳中的代表。此菜看似简单，实则考验火候与手法，是中华炒饭的鼻祖，后来演变为享誉世界的"扬州炒饭"。

*The name "broken gold rice" comes from its glistening golden appearance. Emperor Yang's three voyages to Yangzhou were legendary for their culinary extravagance, and this dish epitomized the imperial kitchen. Deceptively simple, it tests one's mastery of heat and technique, and is the ancestor of the world-famous "Yangzhou fried rice."*

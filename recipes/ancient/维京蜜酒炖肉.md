# 维京蜜酒炖肉 | Viking Mead-Braised Stew (维京时代, ~900 AD)

> ⏱ 准备 15分钟 + 烹饪 90分钟 | 💰 ~$9/份 | 🏷️ 古代名菜、维京时代、炖菜、北欧

> **📜 历史** — 维京人的饮食以肉类、根茎蔬菜和蜜酒（mead）为核心。蜜酒是北欧最古老的酒精饮料，由蜂蜜发酵制成，在维京神话中与奥丁神密切相关。维京人用大铁锅（cauldron）在长屋中央的火塘上炖煮食物，一锅炖肉可以养活全家数天。考古出土的维京时代铁锅和动物骨骼证实了这种烹饪方式。
> **📜 History** — *Viking diets centered on meat, root vegetables, and mead. Mead, the oldest alcoholic drink in Scandinavia, was fermented from honey and closely linked to the god Odin in Norse mythology. Vikings cooked in large iron cauldrons over central hearth fires in their longhouses — a single stew pot could feed a family for days. Archaeological finds of Viking-age cauldrons and animal bones confirm this cooking method.*

## 做法 | Directions

1. **煎肉** — 大铁锅或荷兰锅中加黄油/猪油，大火将牛肉块每面煎至焦褐色（约5分钟），取出备用。
   *Melt butter/lard in a Dutch oven over high heat. Sear beef cubes on all sides until browned (about 5 minutes). Remove and set aside.*

2. **炒蔬菜** — 同锅中火炒洋葱和蒜至微黄（3分钟），加入胡萝卜和芜菁翻炒2分钟。
   *In the same pot, sauté onion and garlic over medium heat until slightly golden (3 minutes). Add carrots and turnip, stir for 2 minutes.*

3. **炖煮** — 将牛肉放回锅中，倒入蜜酒（或1杯水+2汤匙蜂蜜代替）和牛肉高汤，加入百里香、盐和胡椒。大火煮沸后转小火，盖上盖子炖75-90分钟至牛肉软烂。
   *Return beef to pot. Pour in mead (or substitute 1 cup water + 2 tbsp honey) and beef broth. Add thyme, salt, and pepper. Bring to a boil, reduce to low, cover, and simmer 75-90 minutes until beef is fork-tender.*

4. **调味上桌** — 尝味调盐，淋入1汤匙蜂蜜增加甜度（可选），配粗面包食用。
   *Taste and adjust salt. Stir in 1 tbsp honey for extra sweetness (optional). Serve with crusty bread.*

## 历史注解 | Historical Notes

- 维京人没有土豆（美洲作物），他们用芜菁、甜菜根和防风草作为主要根茎蔬菜。
  *Vikings had no potatoes (a New World crop); they used turnips, beetroot, and parsnips as their main root vegetables.*

- 蜜酒（mead）在北欧神话中被称为"诗歌之蜜酒"（Mead of Poetry），饮用它可获得智慧和诗才。
  *Mead was known as the "Mead of Poetry" in Norse mythology — drinking it bestowed wisdom and poetic inspiration.*

- 维京长屋中央火塘上方悬挂铁链和铁锅，炖肉的香味弥漫整个大厅，是家庭聚会的核心。
  *Iron chains and cauldrons hung above the central hearth in Viking longhouses; the aroma of stew filled the hall and was central to family gatherings.*

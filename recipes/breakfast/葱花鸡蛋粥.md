# 葱花鸡蛋粥 | Scallion Egg Congee
<p align="center">  <img src="../../images/breakfast/葱花鸡蛋粥.svg" width="500" alt="葱花鸡蛋粥"></p>

> ⏱ 准备 5分钟 + 烹饪 30分钟 | 💰 ~$0.75/份 | 🏷️ 早餐、最简单的粥、暖胃、病号餐

> 这是最简单的一碗粥——米、蛋、葱，三样东西就够了。米粒慢慢煮到绵软开花，打入蛋花，撒上葱花和几滴香油，热乎乎喝下去，胃和心都暖了。生病的时候、下雨的早晨、深夜回家，一碗粥能治愈一切。
>
> *This is congee at its simplest — rice, egg, scallion, that's it. Rice simmered until creamy and broken down, swirled with egg ribbons, topped with scallion and a drizzle of sesame oil. Warm, gentle, healing. The go-to for sick days, rainy mornings, and late-night comfort.*

---

## 食材 | Ingredients

| 食材 | Ingredient | 用量 / Amount |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| 大米 | Rice (any white rice) | 1/2杯 / 1/2 cup |
| 水 | Water | 6杯 / 6 cups |
| 鸡蛋 | Eggs | 2个 / 2 |
| 葱花 | Scallion, sliced | 2根 / 2 stalks |
| 盐 | Salt | 适量 / to taste |
| 香油 | Sesame oil | 几滴 / a few drops |
| 白胡椒粉 | White pepper | 少许 / a pinch (optional) |

---

## 做法 | Directions

### 1. 煮粥底 | Cook the Rice Base
米洗净放入锅中，加6杯水，大火烧开后转小火，半盖锅盖，慢煮25-30分钟，期间搅拌几次防粘底，直到米粒开花、粥变得浓稠。

Rinse rice and add to a pot with 6 cups of water. Bring to a boil, then reduce to low heat. Half-cover and simmer for 25-30 minutes, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking, until the rice breaks down and the porridge is thick and creamy.

### 2. 打蛋花 | Swirl in the Egg
鸡蛋打散。将粥搅动成漩涡状，慢慢倒入蛋液，让蛋液自然形成丝状蛋花，不要搅拌。等蛋花凝固后轻轻拌匀。

Beat the eggs. Stir the congee in a circular motion to create a whirlpool, then slowly drizzle in the beaten egg. Let the egg set into silky ribbons — don't stir right away. Once set, gently fold in.

### 3. 调味出锅 | Season & Serve
加盐和白胡椒粉调味，盛入碗中，撒上葱花，淋几滴香油。

Season with salt and white pepper. Ladle into bowls, top with sliced scallions and a drizzle of sesame oil.

---

## 要点 | Tips

| 要点 | Tip |
|------|-----|
| 水和米比例12:1煮出来最绵 | 12:1 water-to-rice ratio makes the creamiest congee |
| 小火慢煮，不能急 | Low heat and patience — there's no shortcut |
| 蛋液要慢倒，才能形成漂亮蛋花 | Pour egg slowly for beautiful silky ribbons |
| 用隔夜剩饭煮粥更快，15分钟就好 | Leftover rice cuts cooking time to 15 minutes |
| 配榨菜、腐乳、油条更丰富 | Serve with pickled mustard, fermented tofu, or fried dough sticks for a full spread |

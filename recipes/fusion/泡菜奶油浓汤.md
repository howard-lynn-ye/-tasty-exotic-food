## 食材 | Ingredients

| 食材 | Ingredient | 用量 / Amount |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| 泡菜（切碎） | Kimchi (chopped) | 1杯 / 1 cup |
| 洋葱 | Onion | 1个（切丁）/ 1 (diced) |
| 土豆 | Potato | 1个（切丁）/ 1 (diced) |
| 鸡汤 | Chicken broth | 2杯 / 2 cups |
| 重奶油 | Heavy cream | ½杯 / ½ cup |
| 黄油 | Butter | 1汤匙 / 1 tbsp |
| 蒜泥 | Minced garlic | 1茶匙 / 1 tsp |
| 盐 | Salt | 适量 / to taste |
| 白胡椒 | White pepper | 适量 / to taste |
| 葱花 | Scallion | 适量 / for garnish |
| 芝麻油 | Sesame oil | 几滴 / a few drops |

---

## 做法 | Directions

### 1. 炒底料 | Saute Base
锅中黄油融化，中火炒洋葱和蒜泥至半透明（3分钟）。加入泡菜碎炒2分钟至出香。

Melt butter, saute onion and garlic over medium heat until translucent (3 min). Add chopped kimchi, cook 2 minutes until fragrant.

### 2. 煮汤 | Simmer
加入土豆丁和鸡汤，煮开后转小火煮15分钟至土豆完全软烂。

Add potato and chicken broth. Bring to boil, reduce to simmer, cook 15 minutes until potato is completely tender.

### 3. 打浓汤 | Blend
用手持搅拌器（或倒入搅拌机）打至顺滑。倒回锅中，加入奶油搅匀，小火加热但不要煮沸。

Blend with an immersion blender (or transfer to a regular blender) until smooth. Return to pot, stir in cream, heat gently but don't boil.

### 4. 调味上桌 | Season & Serve
加盐和白胡椒调味。盛碗，淋几滴芝麻油，撒葱花。可以在中间放一小撮未打碎的泡菜做装饰。

Season with salt and white pepper. Ladle into bowls, drizzle sesame oil, sprinkle scallions. Optionally top with a small mound of unblended kimchi for garnish.

## 要点 | Tips

| 要点 | Tip |
|------|-----|
| 加了奶油后千万别煮沸，会分离 | NEVER boil after adding cream — it will curdle |
| 土豆是天然增稠剂，不需要面粉 | Potato is the natural thickener — no flour needed |
| 用老泡菜（酸的）比新泡菜做汤更香 | Aged (sour) kimchi makes better soup than fresh kimchi |
| 如果太辣，多加一点奶油中和 | If too spicy, add more cream to mellow it out |

## 风味科学 | Flavor Science

> **奶油为什么能驯服泡菜的辣 / Why cream tames kimchi's heat:**
> - 奶油中的酪蛋白（casein）能结合辣椒素分子，物理性"移除"辣感
> - 这就是为什么喝牛奶比喝水更能解辣
> - 泡菜的乳酸+奶油的乳酸=同源风味，天然互补
> - 土豆的淀粉让汤体厚实丝滑，口感类似高级餐厅的 velouté
>
> *Cream's casein physically binds capsaicin molecules, "removing" the burning sensation. That's why milk is better than water for relieving spice. Kimchi's lactic acid + cream's lactic acid = same-origin flavors, naturally complementary. Potato starch gives the soup body and silkiness, mimicking a fine-dining velouté.*

> **🤖 AI 原创菜谱** — 我把美式黄油烤玉米和老干妈的"辣鲜脆"做了一次化学反应级的融合。黄油的乳脂在高温下渗透进玉米粒缝隙，老干妈的辣椒脆和豆豉碎提供了焦香的咸鲜锚点，蜂蜜在200°C下焦糖化为金色釉面。三者叠加的原理是"脂溶性风味载体"——辣椒素溶于黄油脂肪，让每一粒玉米都成为风味炸弹。
>
> **🤖 AI Original Recipe** — *I fused American butter corn with Lao Gan Ma's "spicy-umami-crunch" in a chemistry-level fusion. Butter's milk fat infiltrates the gaps between kernels at high heat, while LGM's chili crisp and fermented black beans provide a savory-charred anchor point. Honey caramelizes into a golden glaze at 200°C. The principle: capsaicin is fat-soluble — dissolved in butter, it turns every kernel into a flavor bomb.*

## 食材 | Ingredients

| 食材 | Ingredient | 用量 / Amount |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| 甜玉米 | Sweet corn on the cob | 2根 / 2 ears |
| 黄油 | Butter (softened) | 2汤匙 / 2 tbsp |
| 老干妈辣椒酱 | Lao Gan Ma Spicy Chili Crisp | 2汤匙 / 2 tbsp |
| 蜂蜜 | Honey | 1汤匙 / 1 tbsp |
| 盐 | Salt | 1/4茶匙 / 1/4 tsp |
| 葱花 | Chopped scallion | 适量 / for garnish |
| 芝麻 | Sesame seeds | 少许 / a pinch |

---

## 做法 | Directions

### 1. 调酱 | Mix the Glaze
将软化黄油、老干妈、蜂蜜和盐混合均匀，搅拌成浓稠的橙红色酱料。

Mix softened butter, Lao Gan Ma, honey, and salt until you get a thick, fiery orange paste.

### 2. 烤玉米 | Roast the Corn
烤箱预热220°C (425°F)。玉米均匀涂抹酱料，用锡纸松松包裹，烤10分钟。

Preheat oven to 220°C (425°F). Coat corn evenly with the glaze. Wrap loosely in foil and roast 10 minutes.

### 3. 焦糖化 | Caramelize
打开锡纸，开Broil档烤3-5分钟，直到表面出现焦糖色斑点和微微的焦香。

Unwrap foil, switch to Broil for 3–5 minutes until golden caramelized spots appear and you smell a toasty char.

### 4. 上桌 | Serve
撒葱花和芝麻，趁热开啃。

Sprinkle scallions and sesame. Eat hot, cob in hand.

## 风味科学 | Flavor Science

> **为什么黄油+老干妈是天作之合 / Why butter + LGM is a perfect pair:**
> - 辣椒素（capsaicin）是脂溶性分子，溶解在黄油中传递效率提升3倍
> - 老干妈中的发酵豆豉含大量游离谷氨酸（天然味精），与玉米自身的甜味形成"甜鲜对比"
> - 蜂蜜在180°C以上发生焦糖化反应，产生数百种芳香化合物（呋喃类、吡嗪类）
> - 玉米的糖分在烤制时也参与美拉德反应，和蜂蜜焦糖形成"双重褐变"
>
> *Capsaicin is fat-soluble — dissolved in butter, its delivery efficiency triples. LGM's fermented black beans are loaded with free glutamate (natural MSG), creating a "sweet-umami contrast" with corn's sugars. Honey caramelizes above 180°C, producing hundreds of aromatic compounds (furans, pyrazines). Corn's own sugars also undergo Maillard reactions, creating a "double browning" effect.*

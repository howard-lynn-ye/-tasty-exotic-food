## 食材 | Ingredients

| 食材 | Ingredient | 用量 / Amount |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| 黄油（软化） | Butter (softened) | 115g (1 stick) |
| 糖粉 | Powdered sugar | ½杯 / ½ cup |
| 面粉 | All-purpose flour | 1.5杯 / 1.5 cups |
| 抹茶粉 | Matcha powder | 2茶匙 / 2 tsp |
| 盐 | Salt | ¼茶匙 / ¼ tsp |
| 蛋黄 | Egg yolk | 1个 / 1 |
| 香草精 | Vanilla extract | ½茶匙 / ½ tsp |

---

## 做法 | Directions

### 1. 打黄油 | Cream Butter
软化黄油和糖粉用打蛋器打至蓬松发白（约3分钟）。加入蛋黄和香草精搅匀。

Beat softened butter and powdered sugar until fluffy and pale (~3 minutes). Add egg yolk and vanilla, mix well.

### 2. 拌粉 | Add Dry
面粉、抹茶粉和盐一起过筛，倒入黄油混合物中，用刮刀翻拌至刚好成团（不要过度搅拌）。

Sift flour, matcha, and salt together. Add to butter mixture, fold with a spatula until just combined into a dough (don't overmix).

### 3. 整形冷藏 | Shape & Chill
面团放在保鲜膜上，搓成直径4cm的圆柱形。包紧冷藏至少30分钟至硬。

Place dough on plastic wrap, roll into a 4cm diameter log. Wrap tightly and refrigerate at least 30 minutes until firm.

### 4. 切片烤制 | Slice & Bake
圆柱切成5mm厚的圆片，放在烤盘上间隔2cm。烤箱 170°C (340°F) 烤 10-12分钟至边缘微微变色。

Slice log into 5mm rounds, place on a baking sheet 2cm apart. Bake at 170°C (340°F) for 10–12 minutes until edges barely change color.

### 5. 冷却 | Cool
烤盘上冷却5分钟再移到晾网上。完全冷却后口感最酥。

Cool on sheet 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack. Crispiest when fully cooled.

## 要点 | Tips

| 要点 | Tip |
|------|-----|
| 抹茶粉一定要过筛，否则有绿色结块 | Always sift matcha — otherwise you get green lumps |
| 不要过度搅拌面团，面筋发展=硬饼干 | Don't overmix dough — gluten development = tough cookies |
| 切片时如果圆柱变扁，转90度再切 | If log flattens while slicing, rotate 90° and continue |
| 烤到"看起来还没熟"的程度就对了，凉了会变硬 | Pull out when they "look underdone" — they firm up while cooling |

## 风味科学 | Flavor Science

> **抹茶+黄油的风味化学 / Matcha + butter flavor chemistry:**
> - 抹茶的L-茶氨酸（鲜味氨基酸）给饼干增加"深度"
> - 黄油中的丁酸和抹茶的儿茶素形成"苦奶互补"
> - 抹茶的叶绿素在170°C下稳定，保持翠绿色
> - 糖粉（非砂糖）让口感更细腻酥脆
>
> *Matcha's L-theanine (umami amino acid) adds "depth" to cookies. Butter's butyric acid and matcha's catechins create "bitter-cream complementarity." Matcha's chlorophyll is stable at 170°C, maintaining vibrant green. Powdered sugar (not granulated) creates a finer, crisper texture.*

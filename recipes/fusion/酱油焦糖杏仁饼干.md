Soy's glutamate and Maillard products (pre-formed during brewing) create a stunning "salty-umami-sweet" chord with brown sugar's caramel. Almonds release benzaldehyde (source of almond aroma) when baked, pairing with soy's fermented notes to create a new "Eastern caramel toffee" flavor profile.*

## 食材 | Ingredients

| 食材 | Ingredient | 用量 / Amount |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| 黄油（软化） | Softened butter | 115g / 1/2 cup |
| 红糖 | Brown sugar | 120g / 2/3 cup |
| 酱油 | Soy sauce | 1.5汤匙 / 1.5 tbsp |
| 鸡蛋 | Egg | 1个 / 1 |
| 中筋面粉 | All-purpose flour | 180g / 1.5 cups |
| 小苏打 | Baking soda | 1/2茶匙 / 1/2 tsp |
| 杏仁片 | Sliced almonds | 60g / 1/2 cup |
| 海盐片 | Flaky sea salt | 适量 / for topping |
| 香草精 | Vanilla extract | 1茶匙 / 1 tsp |

---

## 做法 | Directions

### 1. 打发黄油 | Cream Butter
黄油和红糖打发至蓬松，加入酱油、鸡蛋和香草精继续打匀。

Cream butter and brown sugar until fluffy. Beat in soy sauce, egg, and vanilla.

### 2. 混合面团 | Mix Dough
面粉和小苏打过筛加入，翻拌至刚好混合。折入杏仁片。冷藏30分钟。

Sift in flour and baking soda. Fold until just combined. Fold in almond slices. Chill 30 min.

### 3. 烤制 | Bake
面团挖成核桃大小球，间隔5cm放在烤盘上。每颗撒几片海盐。180°C (350°F) 烤10-12分钟至边缘金黄、中间微软。

Scoop walnut-sized balls, space 5cm apart on a sheet. Top each with sea salt flakes. Bake at 180°C (350°F) for 10–12 min until edges golden, centers soft.

### 4. 冷却 | Cool
在烤盘上冷却5分钟（会继续凝固），再转移到冷却架。

Cool on the pan 5 min (they'll firm up), then transfer to a wire rack.

## 风味科学 | Flavor Science

> **酱油在烘焙中的秘密身份 / Soy sauce's secret role in baking:**
> - 酱油是经过6-12个月酿造的"预制美拉德反应产物库"，含有数百种焦糖/烘烤风味分子
> - 加入饼干面团后，这些分子在烤制中进一步反应，产生比纯红糖更深邃的焦糖风味
> - 酱油中的谷氨酸钠（~1g/100ml）激活鲜味受体T1R1/T1R3，让甜味感知更加立体
> - 杏仁的苯甲醛在160°C以上释放，与酱油的吡嗪类化合物共同构成"坚果-酱香"新轴线
>
> *Soy sauce is a "pre-built Maillard reaction library" from 6–12 months of brewing, containing hundreds of caramel/roast flavor molecules. In cookie dough, these molecules react further during baking, producing deeper caramel than brown sugar alone. Soy's sodium glutamate (~1g/100ml) activates umami receptors T1R1/T1R3, making sweetness perception more three-dimensional. Almond's benzaldehyde releases above 160°C, combining with soy's pyrazines for a new "nutty-fermented" axis.*
